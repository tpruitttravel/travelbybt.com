Booking Process

How Our Planning Process Works

We keep your vacation planning simple, transparent, and stress-free—from your first message to the moment you’re back home with photos and stories.

Whether you’re booking a cruise, an all-inclusive resort, a Europe itinerary, or a group celebration trip, the process is the same. Here’s exactly what to expect when you work with Travel by B & T.

Step-by-Step

The Three-Step Planning Experience

1. Connect

It starts with a quick connection. You can complete our trip inquiry form, email, text, or message us on Facebook. We’ll gather your dates, destination ideas, budget, and any must-haves or deal-breakers.

This isn’t a sales pitch—it’s a conversation to understand what a successful trip looks like for you.

2. Curate

Using your preferences, we research and build curated options that actually fit what you asked for—no giant list of random links.

We’ll walk through pros and cons, important fine print, payment schedules, and deadlines so there are no surprises later.

3. Confirm & Go

Once you choose your favorite option, we handle the booking and confirmations—cabins or rooms, transfers (where applicable), and key details.

You’ll receive clear documentation and reminders before you travel. If something comes up before or during your trip, you reach out to us—not a random call center.

What to Expect

Working With Travel by B & T

Communication

We keep you updated as we research options, place holds, and finalize reservations. You’ll know what we’re waiting on and what’s coming next.

Transparency

In many cases, our services are built into the supplier pricing, so you’re not paying more than booking direct. If a planning fee is needed for highly complex itineraries, we’ll discuss that up front before any work begins.

Support

From first quote to final boarding, we act as your advocate. We leverage our partnership with Gateway Travel and preferred suppliers to help keep things on track—and assist when they don’t go as planned.

Flexibility

Not ready to book yet? That’s fine. You can start the planning process to understand options and pricing, then move forward when the timing and budget are right.
