Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to Common Travel Questions

We’ve gathered the most common questions clients ask when planning trips, cruises, and all-inclusive travel through Travel by B & T.

Do you charge any planning fees?

For most trips — including cruises and all-inclusive resorts — we do not charge planning fees. Certain highly customized or complex itineraries involving multiple destinations, rail, or in-country transfers may require a planning retainer. We’ll always tell you upfront.

Why should I use a travel advisor instead of booking myself?

Advisors simplify the process, research options for you, manage documents, communicate with suppliers, and support you before, during, and after travel — at no extra cost for most bookings.

Do you work with all cruise lines?

We book all major ocean cruise lines including Royal Caribbean, Carnival, MSC, Celebrity, Norwegian, Princess, and more — along with river cruise options depending on destination and availability.

What information do you need to start planning?

Basic details like travel dates, number of travelers, destination preferences, budget range, and any must-haves help us curate a tailored list of options.

What forms of payment are accepted?

All payments are made securely through the travel supplier (cruise line, resort, airline). We never store or directly process your payment.

What documents do I need for a cruise?

A valid passport is recommended. Some closed-loop Caribbean cruises allow birth certificate + government-issued ID, but restrictions apply — and passports are always safer.

How do I protect my trip?

Travel insurance is highly recommended and protects against cancellations, delays, medical emergencies, and more.

Do you help with group travel?

Absolutely — from family reunions to birthday trips to large group cruises. We handle the coordination, deadlines, and communications.

Can you help if something goes wrong during travel?

Yes. If a delay, cancellation, or itinerary issue happens, we support you and work with the supplier to navigate options or adjustments.

How do I get started?

The easiest way is our inquiry form — low pressure, quick, and gives us what we need to begin.
