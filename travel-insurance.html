Travel Insurance: Why It Matters

Travel insurance gives you financial protection before and during your trip. We highly recommend insurance for all travelers—especially for cruises, international travel, group trips, and any itinerary with flights.

Why You Need Travel Insurance

  • Trip cancellation due to illness, injury, or personal emergencies
  • Trip interruption or delays
  • Lost or delayed luggage
  • Medical emergencies while traveling
  • Emergency evacuation (very costly without insurance)
  • Cruise-specific protections for itinerary changes or missed ports

Common Coverage Options

  • Trip Cancellation – refunds if you cannot travel for covered reasons
  • Trip Interruption – reimbursement if you must cut your trip short
  • Travel Delay – meals, lodging, and rebooking assistance
  • Medical Coverage – essential for international trips
  • Emergency Medical Evacuation
  • Baggage Loss/Delay
  • "Cancel for Any Reason" (CFAR) – optional upgrade on certain plans

Cruise Travel Insurance

Cruise itineraries are especially vulnerable to weather, port changes, flight delays, and medical issues at sea. Insurance protects your investment and ensures you have access to proper medical care.

How to Purchase Insurance

As travel advisors, we can recommend trusted insurance providers and match you with the right plan based on your trip type and destination. Insurance must be purchased within a specific timeframe after deposit to receive maximum coverage options.

Important Note

Travel insurance policies are provided by third-party insurance carriers. Coverage, claims, and refunds are governed by the insurer’s policy terms—not by Travel by B & T.
